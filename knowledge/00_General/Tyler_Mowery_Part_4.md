The modern resurgence of blackout poetry is often credited to writer Austin Kleon,
whose 2010 book Newspaper Blackout helped popularize the form for a contemporary
audience. Kleon’s work demonstrated how everyday materials like newspapers could be
transformed into striking, minimalist poems through redaction.
Around the same time, the broader literary movement of erasure poetry was gaining
recognition, with poets like Tom Phillips (A Humument) and Ronald Johnson (Radi
Os) using existing texts as raw material for entirely new works.
